Heat First: A Geothermal Agricultural Services Enterprise

Heat First is a proposal for a heat-led geothermal enterprise located in the highland coffee and agricultural communities of West Java, Indonesia. The enterprise uses direct geothermal heat to provide farmers with coffee drying, cold storage and agro-processing services, and pairs these with a 1 MW binary cycle generator that supplies electricity to approximately 500 local households and sells excess power to PLN through a power purchase agreement. We are requesting a USD 2 million concessional loan from the Asian Development Bank (ADB) to co-finance this project alongside commercial debt and equity, and a long-term power purchase agreement (PPA) from PLN to provide the revenue certainty required to unlock that financing.
