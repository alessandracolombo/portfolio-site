Climate Adaptation Plan for Phoenix

Introductory Excerpt

Phoenix is the hottest large city in the United States, and one of the most water-stressed. With over 130 days above 100°F annually, a rapidly expanding urban heat island driven in part by 176 data centers, and a Colorado River supply under mounting strain, the city faces compounding climate risks that need coordinated, locally-controlled action. This project analyzes Phoenix's current vulnerabilities and proposes two strategies: a shade expansion program that builds on the existing Shade Phoenix Plan, and a bioswale and water retention network to capture monsoon rainfall. Part of the proposal was a financial model to create a Climate Resilience Fund to secure funding for these initiatives.
